Resources & Sustainability

Renewable vs Non-Renewable

Renewable Resources

Resources that naturally replenish over time when managed sustainably.

Examples:

  • Solar Energy
  • Wind Energy
  • Water from rivers and rainfall
  • Timber from forests

How Renewable Works

The sun shines daily, wind blows regularly, and forests grow new trees.

India has abundant sunshine and regular rainfall that feeds rivers and replenishes soil.

Key: Must be used at the rate nature can replace them.

The Challenge

If we harvest faster than nature can replace, we lose them.

Example: Cutting trees faster than forests can grow = deforestation.

Climate change is melting glaciers faster than rain can replace them, threatening water security.

Non-Renewable Resources

Resources that take millions of years to form and cannot be replaced once used.

Examples:

  • Coal
  • Petroleum (Oil)
  • Natural minerals (Gold, Copper, Iron)

Limited Time

India's coal reserves may only last another 50 years at current usage rates.

We must use them wisely until sustainable alternatives are widely available.

The choice is clear: shift toward renewable resources for a sustainable future.
